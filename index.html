Original Case Study

How to Improve Nigerian Fintech Apps

Based on 6+ years of experience in payment operations, wallet product development, and customer escalation handling — five recurring failures and the specific product fixes that address them.

01
Error States Designed for Engineers
Users see raw gateway error codes instead of plain-language explanations of what happened and what to do next.
→ Write user-friendly copy for every failure state. Always tell users what happened to their money.
02
Escrow UX Creates Anxiety
Escrow is a trust product, but most implementations leave both buyer and seller confused about who holds the money and for how long.
→ Real-time status visibility, proactive push notifications, prominent confirm-receipt CTA.
03
Onboarding Kills Activation
Full KYC requirements front-loaded before users have experienced any product value. Most users abandon before step 4.
→ Progressive KYC tiers. Let users receive money and explore before requiring ID verification.
04
Wallet-to-Bank Abandonment
Bank selection, name lookup, and failure handling are poorly designed — especially on slow connections.
→ Searchable bank list, loading indicators, name lookup caching, saved beneficiaries.
05
Trust Built Too Late
Regulatory credentials and security signals buried in Terms & Conditions or the About page — where no one sees them.
→ Embed trust signals in the product flow: during KYC, at first transaction, on the wallet home screen.
